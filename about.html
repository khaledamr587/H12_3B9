About Sufes

Sufes, located near Sbiba in central Tunisia, was a thriving Roman-Berber city with a rich history spanning back to the 2nd century BCE. Known for its strategic location, the city flourished under Roman rule, with impressive structures like the Capitolium, theaters, and baths. The influence of Roman, Berber, and Phoenician cultures is evident in its architecture and urban planning. Over time, natural disasters and invasions led to its decline, leaving behind valuable archaeological remnants.
